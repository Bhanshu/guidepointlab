Test: CRP – C-Reactive Protein
  • Other Names:

    CRP, C-Reactive Protein

  • Conditions Associated with the Test:
    • Inflammation
    • Heart Disease
    • Infection
    • Autoimmune Disease
  • Why get tested?

    The CRP or C-Reactive Protein is a non-specific marker for inflammation. It is associated with injury to tissues and activation of the inflammatory pathways in the blood. It is not used to diagnose a specific disease but is useful in decision making in patients with other disorders involving inflammation. The CRP is also used to monitor changes in treatment in inflammatory diseases such as IBD, Ulcerative Colitis, Chron’s Disease or other autoimmune diseases.

  • How is it used?

    The CRP is used to make decisions about treatment and to monitor response to treatment in inflammatory conditions

  • Why is it ordered?

    The CRP or C-Reactive Protein is used in patients with conditions that cause chronic inflammation like heart disease, arthritis or autoimmune disease or it is used to monitor response to treatment in conditions involving inflammation like Lupus or Rheumatoid Arthritis.

  • What does the test result mean?

    The CRP level is typically low in the blood. Elevation of CRP indicates ongoing inflammation as may be seen in autoimmune disease, autoinflammatory disease or with chronic infections.

  • What Type of Sample is required?

    One Tube of Blood

  • Do I Need to Be Fasting or Know Anything Else?

    No Fasting or other preparation is necessary
